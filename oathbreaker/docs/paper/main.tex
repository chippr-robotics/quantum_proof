\begin{abstract}
We present an open reversible circuit framework implementing the coherent
group-action map $|a\rangle|b\rangle|O\rangle \to |a\rangle|b\rangle|[a]G + [b]Q\rangle$
for the Elliptic Curve Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP) on the Oath-64 curve
over the Goldilocks field $\mathbb{F}_p$ where $p = 2^{64} - 2^{32} + 1$.
This map is the computationally dominant component of Shor's ECDLP algorithm,
consuming $>$99\% of the qubits and gates.
The circuit's execution trace is proven correct via an SP1 zkVM Groth16 SNARK
and independently verified against classically-computed ground truth via
Pollard's rho algorithm. We report measured resource counts (logical qubits,
Toffoli gates, circuit depth) and project scaling to cryptographically relevant
field sizes. The full circuit is published as a quantum hardware benchmark ---
the Oathbreaker Scale --- which scores quantum computers by the largest Oath
curve they can crack. We explicitly delineate what is proven (classical
arithmetic correctness, reversible consistency, ancilla cleanup) from what is
assumed (superposition behavior) or deferred (QFT, measurement, post-processing).
\end{abstract}
