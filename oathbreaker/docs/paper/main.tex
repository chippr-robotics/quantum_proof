\begin{abstract}
We present a complete implementation of Shor's algorithm for the Elliptic Curve
Discrete Logarithm Problem (ECDLP) on the Oath-64 curve, a toy elliptic curve
over the Goldilocks field $\mathbb{F}_p$ where $p = 2^{64} - 2^{32} + 1$. Unlike prior work that
proves only subroutines (e.g., Google's point addition proof for secp256k1),
our circuit is end-to-end: it encompasses all components of Shor's algorithm
including controlled scalar multiplication, the Quantum Fourier Transform, and
phase estimation. The circuit's correctness is proven via an SP1 zkVM Groth16
SNARK and independently verified against classically-computed ground truth via
Pollard's rho algorithm. We report measured resource counts (qubits, Toffoli
gates, circuit depth) and project scaling to cryptographically relevant field
sizes. The full circuit is published as a quantum hardware benchmark --- the
Oathbreaker Scale --- which scores quantum computers by the largest Oath curve they can crack.
\end{abstract}
